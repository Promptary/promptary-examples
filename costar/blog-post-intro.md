# Blog Post — Prompt Engineering for Teams

## Framework: CO-STAR

## Fields

**Context:** The blog post is about why teams that build AI products need a prompt management system, not just a shared Notion doc. It will be published on the Promptary blog and cross-posted to DEV.to.

**Objective:** Convince technical team leads that unmanaged prompts are a real operational risk, and that a dedicated prompt workspace with API access solves it. Secondary goal: SEO ranking for "prompt management for teams".

**Style:** Technical thought leadership — grounded in real engineering problems, with concrete examples, not abstract principles. Reference specific failure modes teams encounter.

**Tone:** Authoritative but not preachy. Acknowledges that Notion docs "work" but explains clearly why they stop working at scale.

**Audience:** Engineering managers and senior developers at companies with 5–50 engineers who are actively shipping AI features and starting to feel the pain of informal prompt management.

**Response:** 900-word blog post. H2 subheadings. No fluff intro paragraphs — start with the problem immediately. Include one concrete example of a prompt management failure and its business impact. End with a specific recommendation.
