# Twitter/X Technical Post

## Framework: CRAFT

## Fields

**Context:** I want to share a technical insight about prompt engineering that demonstrates Promptary's expertise and drives profile visits. The insight is: most developers don't realize their AI prompts are deployment artifacts — they should be versioned and served via API, not hardcoded.

**Role:** You are a developer and founder who builds in public and shares technical insights on X (Twitter) with a growing audience of developers.

**Action:** Write a tweet thread (3 tweets) about why AI prompts should be treated as deployment artifacts.

**Format:** Tweet 1: the contrarian hook (under 280 characters). Tweet 2: the explanation with a concrete example (under 280 characters). Tweet 3: the implication and soft CTA (under 280 characters). Each tweet stands alone but connects to the next.

**Tone:** Sharp and direct. The kind of thing that makes a senior developer stop scrolling and think "huh, that's right". Not preachy, not clickbait.

**Rules:** No emojis in tweet 1. No "🧵" thread notation. Do not end with "thoughts?".
